I am a PhD student in Computer Science at [Peking University](https://www.pku.edu.cn/), advised by Prof. [Guoping Wang](https://www.eecs.pku.edu.cn/info/1046/7111.htm) at the [Graphics and Interaction Laboratory (GIL)](https://www.graphics.pku.edu.cn/). I am part of the combined Master's-PhD program, having completed my Master's phase under Assoc. Prof. [Yisong Chen](https://www.eecs.pku.edu.cn/info/1046/7111.htm).

My research focuses on **3D computer vision**, with an emphasis on **large-scale 3D reconstruction** and **world models**. I work on building efficient and scalable systems for reconstructing and generating 3D scenes. My recent work includes:

- **3D Gaussian Splatting** for city-scale reconstruction with real-time rendering
- **Multi-temporal 3D scene modeling** that disentangles invariant structures from temporal changes
- **World models** for scene generation from images and text, bridging 2D generative models and 3D representations
- **Distributed systems** for scaling reconstruction pipelines across GPU clusters

Before PKU, I earned my B.S. in Computer Science from [Wuhan University](https://www.whu.edu.cn/) (2019–2023), where I was a competitive programmer — winning a gold medal at the ACM-ICPC Asia Regional Contest and a bronze at CCF NOI.

**I am actively looking for research internships** in 3D vision, generative models, and world models.
